They describe how Heidi’s predisposition to wander and learn was later evident in her behavior". (Honey, Dwyer & Iliescu, 2020a)

HeiDI is a new computational model of Pavlovian learning and performance with reciprocal associations. The name of the model, reflects the literary reference and links the authors’ surnames to one of the principal issues that the model seeks to address: How excitation and inhibition Determine Ideo-motion. HeiDI enables a broad range of phenomena to be accommodated, which are either beyond the scope of extant models or require them to appeal to additional (learning) processes. It also provides an impetus for new lines of inquiry and generates novel predictions.

> HeiDI equations <

The full equations for the HeiDI model are given in the relevant papers. In brief, they are:

ΔVCS-US = αCS (c * βUS - ΣVTOTAL-US) (1)
ΔVUS-CS = βCS (c * αUS - ΣVTOTAL-CS) (2)
VCOMB = VCS-US + (1/c * VCS-US * VUS-CS ) (3a)
VCOMB-AB = ΣVAB-US + [1⁄c * VAB-US * (VUS-A + VUS-B)] (3b)
RCS = [αCS ⁄ (αCS + 1⁄c * |VCS-US| )] * VCOMB (4)
RUS = [(1⁄c * |VCS-US| ) ⁄ (αCS + 1⁄c * |VCS-US| )] * VCOMB (5)
r1 = (1⁄c* RCS * VCS-r1) + (1⁄c* RUS * VUS-r1) (6)
