Excitatory conditioning

These simulations accompany the ones sumarrised in Honey, Dwyer & Iliescu (2020a; pp 837-838; Figure 3 and Figure 4). It illustrates HeiDI's predictions for the formation of CS-US links (VCS-US, VUS-CS), their combination (VCOMB), and how this translates into response tendencies directed towards the CS (RCS) and US (RUS) during a simple Pavlovian conditioning design (continous reinformcent of a conditioned stimulus, CS+).

Panel A depicts simulations of the development of the CS-US associations, derived from Equation 1 (VCS-US), the US-CS associations, derived from Equation 2 (VUS-CS), and their combined values, generated from Equation 3 (VCOMB). Maximum VCS-US is determined by βUS and maximum VUS-CS is determined by αCS. As noted in Honey et al., (2020a), the CS-US association (VCS-US) approaches the asymptote derived from the βUS value less quickly when αCS is low compared to when it is higher. Similarly, VUS-CS associations, derived from Equation 2 (VUS-CS), approach the asymptote derived from the αCS value less readily when βUS is set at a lower value is low compared to when it is higher.

The combination of these two associations (VCS-US and VUS-CS) is noted as VCOMB (Equation 3). Equation 3 weights VCS-US more than VUS-CS because VCS-US constrains the impact of VUS-CS .

Panel B illustrates how Equations 4 and 5 distribute into RCS and RUS values across a series of CS-US pairings. This provides proof-of-principle that variation in the relative values of αCS and βUS affect changes in RCS and RUS that mirror qualitative individual differences in conditioned behaviour (e.g. sign-tracking vs. goal-tracking).

Functionality

Simulations tab

HeiDI's predictions can be explored using the three sliders.

Sliders

  • Number of trials (range 1 - 100) - Sets the number of trials desired.
  • α (range 0.1 - 1) - Sets the perceived value for the CS.
  • β (range 0.1 - 1) - Sets the perceived value for the US.

Buttons

  • button - Link to code of a standalone version, in R, on github.
  • button - downloads the csv file generated in the simulation. It corresponds to the same data that be visualised in the Data tab

Data tab

Every time sliders are adjusted, the simulation dataframe is re-created. This tab shows the data plotted in the figures.
