Compound conditioning and the pooled error term

These simulations accompany the ones summarrised in Honey, Dwyer and Iliescu (2020a; pp 840-841; Figure 7). Rescorla (2000) first trained two of the excitors (A and C) and two inhibitors (B and D) to asymptote. Subsequently, A and B are presented in a compound and reinforced (AB+). The results showed the excitatory A changed less than the inhibitory B when the compound was reinforced. However, the fact that HeiDI explicitly includes reciprocal CS-US and US-CS associations implies that the impact of compound conditioning on the backwards associations need to be considered: namely those those between the US and A , and between US and B . The design of the simulated data is summarised in Table 1. VUS-A will be equal to α A by the end of the first stage of training, but VUS-B will be 0, because B has not been paired with the US . Thus, during pairings of AB with the US , VUS-A will not change (the asymptote for VUS-A will have been reached as a result of the first stage of training), however VUS-B can increase (to the asymptote set by α B). As a result of this, VCOMB-BC will be higher than VCOMB-AD. The key to this analysis is that, despite the used of a pooled error term in Equations 1 and 2, associations from the US to A and to B will be formed independently when there is a single US.

Thus HeiDI is able to reconcile the use of a pooled error terms with the observation that stimuli with different associative histories appear to display different amounts of associative change when they are trained together.

Functionality

Simulations tab

HeiDI's predictions can be explored using the three sliders.

Sliders

  • Number of trials (range 1 - 100) - Sets the number of trials desired. The slider only allows even numbers, as the simulations assume equal number of A+ and AB- trials.
  • α A (range 0.1 - 1) - Sets the perceived CS value for stimulus A.
  • α B (range 0.1 - 1) - Sets the perceived CS value for the stimulus B.
  • β (range 0.1 - 1) - Sets the perceived value for the US.

Buttons

  • button - Link to code of a standalone version, in R, on github.
  • button - downloads the csv file generated in the simulation. It corresponds to the same data that be visualised in the Data tab

Data tab

Every time sliders are adjusted, the simulation dataframe is re-created. This tab shows the data plotted in the figures.
